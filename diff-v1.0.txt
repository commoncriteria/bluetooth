										      >	1 Introduction {#Introduction .indexable data-level="1"}
										      >	==============
										      >
										      >	1.1 Overview {#ppoverview .indexable data-level="2"}
										      >	------------
										      >
										      >	The scope of the Bluetooth [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) is to describe
										      >	the security functionality of Bluetooth technology in terms of
										      >	\[[CC](#abbr_CC)\] and to define functional and assurance requirements
										      >	for the Bluetooth capability of mobile devices and operating systems.
										      >	Bluetooth is a communications standard for short-range wireless
										      >	transmissions. Bluetooth is implemented in many commercial devices as a
										      >	method for wirelessly connecting devices or accessories. This
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) is intended for use with the following
										      >	Base-PPs:
										      >
										      >	-   General Purpose Operating System (GPOS) Protection Profile, Version
										      >	    4.2.1
										      >	-   Mobile Device Fundamentals ([MDF](#abbr_MDF)) Protection Profile,
										      >	    Version 3.2
										      >
										      >	These Base-PPs are valid because consumer-grade desktop and mobile
										      >	devices may both have Bluetooth hardware radios and so both desktop and
										      >	mobile operating systems have the software/firmware capability to allow
										      >	products to use them.
										      >

										      >	1.3 Compliant Targets of Evaluation {#TOEdescription .indexable data-level="2"}
										      >	-----------------------------------
										      >
										      >	The Target of Evaluation ([TOE](#abbr_TOE)) in this
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) is a product that implements Bluetooth
										      >	functionality. This [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) describes the extended
										      >	security functionality of Bluetooth in terms of [CC](#abbr_CC). This
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) extends the Protection Profile for General
										      >	Purpose Operating Systems or Mobile Device Fundamentals. A compliant
										      >	[TOE](#abbr_TOE) will meet all mandatory SFRs defined in this
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) in addition to the mandatory SFRs of its
										      >	claimed [Base-PP](#abbr_Base-PP). For each [Base-PP](#abbr_Base-PP),
										      >	this [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) refines several of the
										      >	[Base-PP](#abbr_Base-PP)\'s SFRs so that they can accommodate the
										      >	Bluetooth functionality defined by the [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module). A
										      >	compliant [TOE](#abbr_TOE) will claim all selection-based SFRs from this
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) and its [Base-PP](#abbr_Base-PP) as needed
										      >	based on the relevant selections in other requirements being chosen.
										      >
										      >	Note that [\[MDF\]](#bibMDF) evaluation activities require certain tests
										      >	to be performed against all radios present on the device. When the
										      >	[TOE](#abbr_TOE) also claims conformance to a
										      >	[PP-Configuration](#abbr_PP-Configuration) that includes this
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module), those tests are executed against the
										      >	Bluetooth radio as well.
										      >
										      >	Also note that each [Base-PP](#abbr_Base-PP) defines its own
										      >	requirements for protection of data at rest. When the [TOE](#abbr_TOE)
										      >	also claims conformance to a [PP-Configuration](#abbr_PP-Configuration)
										      >	that includes this [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module), any data that is used
										      >	by the [TOE](#abbr_TOE)\'s Bluetooth implementation is expected to be
										      >	stored using the same protection mechanisms.
										      >
										      >	### 1.3.1 TOE Boundary {#TOEboundary .indexable data-level="3"}
										      >
										      >	The Bluetooth implementation is a logical component executing on an end
										      >	user personal computing or mobile device. As such, the [TOE](#abbr_TOE)
										      >	must rely heavily on the [TOE](#abbr_TOE)\'s operational environment
										      >	(host platform, network stack, and operating system) for its execution
										      >	domain and its proper usage. The [TOE](#abbr_TOE) will rely on the IT
										      >	environment to address much of the security functionality related to
										      >	administrative functions. The physical boundary of the [TOE](#abbr_TOE)
										      >	includes the physical device on which it is installed, as this device
										      >	will contain an internal or external Bluetooth radio that is used as the
										      >	physical medium for transmitting and receiving data over the Bluetooth
										      >	logical channel.

										      >	-------------
										      >
										      >	Requirements in this [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) are designed to
										      >	address the security problems in at least the following use cases. These
										      >	use cases are intentionally very broad, as many specific use cases exist
										      >	within these larger categories.

										      >	:   This use case is for a Bluetooth [TOE](#abbr_TOE) that is part of a
										      >	    general-purpose operating system. Specifically, the Bluetooth
										      >	    [TOE](#abbr_TOE) is expected to be part of the operating system
										      >	    itself and not a standalone third-party application that is
										      >	    installed on top of it.
										      >
										      >	 \[USE CASE 2\] Mobile Device
										      >	:   This use case is for a Bluetooth [TOE](#abbr_TOE) that is part of a
										      >	    mobile operating system that runs on a mobile device. Specifically,
										      >	    the Bluetooth [TOE](#abbr_TOE) is expected to be part of the mobile
										      >	    operating system itself and not a standalone third-party application
										      >	    that is acquired from the mobile vendor\'s application store.

										      >	    further restricts a requirement.

										      >	    requirement.

										      >	    text]{.assignable-content}): is used to assign a specific value to
										      >	    an unspecified parameter, such as the length of a password. Showing
										      >	    the value in square brackets indicates assignment.
										      >	-   **Iteration** operation: is indicated by appending the
										      >	    [SFR](#abbr_SFR) name with a slash and unique identifier suggesting
										      >	    the purpose of the operation, e.g. \"/EXAMPLE1.\"

										      >	5.2 General Purpose Operating Systems PP Security Functional Requirements Direction {
										      >	-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
										      >
										      >	In a [PP-Configuration](#abbr_PP-Configuration) that includes General
										      >	Purpose Operating Systems [PP](#abbr_PP), the [TOE](#abbr_TOE) is
										      >	expected to rely on some of the security functions implemented by the as
										      >	a whole and evaluated against the General Purpose Operating Systems
										      >	[PP](#abbr_PP). The following sections describe any modifications that
										      >	the [ST](#abbr_ST) author must make to the SFRs defined in the General
										      >	Purpose Operating Systems [PP](#abbr_PP) in addition to what is mandated
										      >	by [Section 5.3 TOE Security Functional
										      >	Requirements](#man-sfrs){.dynref}.
										      >
										      >	### 5.2.1 Modified SFRs {#modsfr-b-gpos .indexable data-level="3"}
										      >
										      >	The SFRs listed in this section are defined in the General Purpose
										      >	Operating Systems [PP](#abbr_PP) and relevant to the secure operation of
										      >	the [TOE](#abbr_TOE).
										      >
										      >	#### 5.2.1.1 Security Management (FMT) {#fmt .indexable data-level="4"}
										      >
										      >	::: {#FMT_MOF_EXT.1 .comp}
										      >	#### FMT\_MOF\_EXT.1 Management of Security Functions Behavior
										      >
										      >	::: {.element}
										      >	::: {#FMT_MOF_EXT.1.1 .reqid}
										      >	[FMT\_MOF\_EXT.1.1](#FMT_MOF_EXT.1.1){.abbr}
										      >	:::
										      >
										      >	::: {.reqdesc}
										      >	There is no change to the text of this [SFR](#abbr_SFR). The
										      >	[SFR](#abbr_SFR) references [FMT\_SMF\_EXT.1](#FMT_SMF_EXT.1) and states
										      >	that the OS shall permit the administrator role to perform the relevant
										      >	functions listed in [FMT\_SMF\_EXT.1](#FMT_SMF_EXT.1). The function
										      >	\"Enable/Disable the Bluetooth interface\" is listed as an optional
										      >	management function in [FMT\_SMF\_EXT.1](#FMT_SMF_EXT.1) for both users
										      >	and administrators. When this [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) is claimed,
										      >	the administrator or user role must be able to enable/disable the
										      >	Bluetooth interface. In other words, the function itself is moved from
										      >	optional to mandatory, but this [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) does not
										      >	require that it be implemented by a specific role. If the [ST](#abbr_ST)
										      >	indicates that the administrator role can perform this function, then
										      >	the restrictions imposed by [FMT\_MOF\_EXT.1](#FMT_MOF_EXT.1) will apply
										      >	to it.
										      >	:::
										      >	:::
										      >	:::

										      >	[FMT\_SMF\_EXT.1.1](#FMT_SMF_EXT.1.1){.abbr}
										      >	:::
										      >
										      >	::: {.reqdesc}
										      >	This [PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) does not modify this [SFR](#abbr_SFR)
										      >	as it is defined in the GPOS [PP](#abbr_PP). However, note that this
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) requires the function \"Enable/disable
										      >	Bluetooth interface\" to be implemented, though this
										      >	[PP-Module](#abbr_PP-Module) does not mandate whether it be assigned to

										      >	#### FCS\_CKM\_EXT.8 Bluetooth Key Generation
										      >
										      >	::: {.element}
										      >	::: {#FCS_CKM_EXT.8.1 .reqid}
										      >	[FCS\_CKM\_EXT.8.1](#FCS_CKM_EXT.8.1){.abbr}
										      >	:::

										      >	::: {.reqdesc}
										      >	The [TSF](#abbr_TSF) shall generate public/private [ECDH](#abbr_ECDH)
										      >	key pairs every \[**assignment**: [frequency of and/or criteria for new
										      >	key pair generation]{.assignable-content}\].
										      >
										      >	::: {.appnote}
										      >	[Application Note: ]{.note-header}[There are multiple acceptable ways of
										      >	keeping [ECDH](#abbr_ECDH) key pairs adequately fresh, including a
										      >	time-based approach such that the same key pairs will not be used for
										      >	more than, for instance, 24 hours. Alternatively, the criteria might be
										      >	linked to the number of passed or failed authentication attempts. As a
										      >	starting point to determine reasonable authentication attempt-based
										      >	replacement criteria, note that the Bluetooth specification (v4.1, Vol.
										      >	2, 5.1) suggests mitigating repeated authentication attempts by changing
										      >	a device\'s private key after three failed authentication attempts from
										      >	any BD\_ADDR, after ten successful pairings from any BD\_ADDR, or after
										      >	a combination of these such that any three successful pairings count as
										      >	one failed pairing. ]{.note}
										      >
										      >	This requirement also applies to Bluetooth [LE](#abbr_LE) if the
										      >	[TOE](#abbr_TOE) supports [LE](#abbr_LE) Secure Connections, which was
										      >	introduced in version 4.2 of the specification.

										      >	### 5.3.3 Identification and Authentication (FIA) {#fia .indexable data-level="3"}
										      >
										      >	::: {#FIA_BLT_EXT.1 .comp}
										      >	#### FIA\_BLT\_EXT.1 Bluetooth User Authorization
										      >
										      >	::: {.element}
										      >	::: {#FIA_BLT_EXT.1.1 .reqid}
										      >	[FIA\_BLT\_EXT.1.1](#FIA_BLT_EXT.1.1){.abbr}
										      >	:::
										      >
										      >	::: {.reqdesc}
										      >	The [TSF](#abbr_TSF) shall require explicit user authorization before
										      >	pairing with a remote Bluetooth device.
										      >
										      >	::: {.appnote}
										      >	[Application Note: ]{.note-header}[ User authorization includes explicit
										      >	actions like affirming the remote device\'s name, expressing an intent
										      >	to connect to the remote device, and entering relevant pairing
										      >	information (e.g. PINs; numeric codes; or \"yes/no\" responses). The
										      >	user must have to explicitly permit all pairing attempts; even when
										      >	bonding is not taking place.]{.note}
										      >
										      >	Because explicit user action must be required to permit pairing; it must
										      >	not be possible for applications to programmatically enter pairing
										      >	information (e.g. PINs; numeric codes; or \"yes/no\" responses) during
										      >	the pairing process. The absence of public APIs for programmatic
										      >	authorization is not sufficient to meet this requirement; hidden or
										      >	private APIs must be absent as well.
										      >	:::
										      >	:::
										      >	:::
										      >	:::
										      >

										      >	:::
										      >
										      >	::: {.reqdesc}
										      >	The [TSF](#abbr_TSF) shall require Bluetooth mutual authentication
										      >	between devices prior to any data transfer over the Bluetooth link.
										      >
										      >	::: {.appnote}
										      >	[Application Note: ]{.note-header}[ If devices are not already paired,
										      >	the pairing process must be initiated. If the devices are already
										      >	paired, mutual authentication based on the current link key must succeed
										      >	before any data passes over the link. ]{.note}
										      >	:::
										      >	:::
										      >	:::
										      >	:::

										      >

										      >	:::
										      >
										      >	::: {.reqdesc}
										      >	The [TSF](#abbr_TSF) shall discard pairing and session initialization
										      >	attempts from a Bluetooth device address (BD\_ADDR) to which an active
										      >	session already exists.
										      >
										      >	::: {.appnote}
										      >	[Application Note: ]{.note-header}[ Session is defined as the time
										      >	interval for which the [TSF](#abbr_TSF) is actively connected to another
										      >	device. Thus, the session terminates when the device disconnects from
										      >	the [TSF](#abbr_TSF). If the [TOE](#abbr_TOE) has an active session to a
										      >	remote Bluetooth device, new session initialization and/or pairing
										      >	attempts from devices claiming the same Bluetooth device address may be
										      >	malicious and should be rejected/ignored. Only one session to a single
										      >	remote BD\_ADDR may be supported at a time. ]{.note}

										      >	[Application Note: ]{.note-header}[ In addition to pairing, it may be
										      >	appropriate to require explicit user action to authorize a particular

										      >	may choose to require this additional action for all devices or only for
										      >	those devices that do not have a required level of trust.]{.note}
										      >
										      >	It is strongly preferred that for each device, the [TSF](#abbr_TSF)

										      >	However, the [TSF](#abbr_TSF) might designate certain devices as having
										      >	a trusted device relationship with the [TOE](#abbr_TOE) and granting
										      >	them \"blanket\" access to all services.

										      >	movement of devices from the untrusted to the trusted category for a
										      >	particular service after the user provides explicit authorization for
										      >	the device to use the service. For example, it may be appropriate to
										      >	require that the user provide explicit, manual authorization before a
										      >	remote device may use the [OBEX](#abbr_OBEX) service for an object

										      >	[TOE](#abbr_TOE) may distinguish between \"trusted\" and \"untrusted\"
										      >	devices such that the [TSF](#abbr_TSF) grants \"untrusted\" devices
										      >	access to fewer services following pairing. However, this behavior is
										      >	not required; if the [TSF](#abbr_TSF) does not treat \"trusted\" and
										      >	\"untrusted\" devices any differently, the [ST](#abbr_ST) author may
										      >	complete the assignments in [FIA\_BLT\_EXT.6.1](#FIA_BLT_EXT.6.1) and
